I am a health systems researcher with a Ph.D. in Marketing, specializing in the intersection of patient experience, business process management, and digital transformation in healthcare. My work focuses on improving health system efficiency and equity, primarily within the contexts of Eastern Europe and developing economies.

My research employs a mixed-methods approach, combining large-scale quantitative patient surveys with in-depth qualitative insights from clinical experts. I am particularly interested in how patient-centered strategies and digital innovations can be harnessed to overcome structural inefficiencies, mitigate personnel deficits, and address access disparities in state-dominated healthcare infrastructures.

**Current Research**

I am currently a Visiting Ph.D. Researcher at the Hungarian University of Agriculture and Life Sciences (MATE) in Gödöllő, Hungary, supported by the prestigious Stipendium Hungaricum program. My work here involves applying advanced economic modeling and predictive statistics to estimate the burden of Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) and identify cost-saving workflows within hospital datasets.

Simultaneously, I am pursuing a Candidate of Economic Sciences degree (Cand. Econ. Sc.) at Southwest State University, Russia, with a thesis focused on "Innovative Business Process Management in the Healthcare System Based on the Development of Digital Technologies." This research stream explores the gap between theoretical digital frameworks and their practical implementation, analyzing topics such as physician attitudes towards digital tools and the phenomenon of "Digital Fatigue" among healthcare workers.

**Core Research Interests**

*   **Health System Efficiency & Financial Modeling:** Analyzing hybrid funding models and their impact on system stability.
*   **Patient Experience & Centeredness:** Applying marketing frameworks (like the 7Ps) to public health to prioritize improvements in service process and personnel competence.
*   **Digital Transformation in Healthcare:** Investigating the strategic prioritization, barriers, and optimization potential of AI, telemedicine, and process automation.
*   **Health Equity:** Addressing geographic and structural disparities in access and trust, particularly the urban/rural divide in Eastern Europe.

My work has been published in Q1 and Q2 journals such as *MDPI Sustainability* and *MDPI Healthcare*, and I regularly present my findings at international conferences. I am committed to producing research that not only advances academic understanding but also provides actionable insights for policymakers and healthcare administrators.
